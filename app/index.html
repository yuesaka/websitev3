About

Yuma Uesaka is a musician, improvisor, composer, and developer from West Bloomfield, Michigan. At the age of ten, he picked up the saxophone and soon started to take interest in jazz. During high school, he was a part of the Detroit Symphony Orchestra’s Civic Jazz Orchestra, where he received tutelage from bassist Rodney Whitaker. At the same time, he spent a good amount of his teenage years composing music through the use of MML, a programming language made for sequencing music on the computer.

In May 2014, Yuma completed his dual degree in Jazz and Contemplative Studies (BFA) and Computer Science Engineering (BSE) at the University of Michigan, where he studied under Robert Hurst, Geri Allen, Andrew Bishop, Ellen Rowe, and Martha Travers. While in Ann Arbor, Yuma performed with a diverse range of musical groups including Phil Ogilvie’s Rhythm Kings, Tree City and the Contraband, Vincent Chandler Septet, and the University of Michigan Jazz Ensemble. As a member of the UM Jazz Ensemble, he has had the privilege of performing with numerous jazz greats such as Lee Konitz, David Liebman, Jeff “Tain” Watts, and Robert Hurst amongst others. In April 2014, Yuma was chosen to play in a jazz quartet the university has sent to Shanghai to perform at Shanghai Jiao Tong University's International Culture Festival. In August 2014, he spent a month in Mysore, India to learn the basics of Carnatic flute playing from one of the local gurus.

Currently, Yuma resides in Brooklyn, New York to immerse himself in the world of music and technology. He spends his time programming, practicing, composing, and meeting creatives in the area.
